## First person

[Cognate set 1abs](CognatesetTable?#cldf:1abs)

* reconstructible: [Form pc-one](FormTable?no_translation#cldf:pc-one)
* parallel innovations:
    * grammaticalization of emphatic marker \**rə*
    * coalescence of *wɨ* to *u*
* appearance of *j* in [Language pem](LanguageTable?#cldf:pem) and [Language pan](LanguageTable?#cldf:pan) -- via /əwɨ/ > /ɨwɨ/ > /ɨu/ > /ju/? ([meira2002first](sources.bib?with_internal_ref_link&ref#cldf:meira2002first): 259)
* additional *hɨ* in Manapiarean

## First person inclusive (1+2)
* etymologically very diverse forms
1. two apparently distinct roots [Cognate set 1a2](CognatesetTable?inline#cldf:1a2) and [Cognate set uku](CognatesetTable?inline#cldf:uku) with same core meaning
	* [Cognate set 1a2](CognatesetTable?inline#cldf:1a2) combines with different formatives
    * even within same language ([Language kar](LanguageTable?#cldf:kar))
2. innovative forms
	* <span class='smallcaps'>1</span>-<span class='smallcaps'>pl</span> in the Pemon-Panare group
    * old infinitive form of copula for Manapiarean
    * unrelated *epɨ* in Yukpan

## First person exclusive (1+3)
[Cognate set 1a3](CognatesetTable?#cldf:1a3)

* related but rather different forms
* [meira2002first](sources.bib?with_internal_ref_link&ref#cldf:meira2002first): 263 reconstructs \**apina* or \**aipna*, with different patterns of loss
* various additional initial elements like /ju/, /t͡ʃi/, /n/

## Shared innovations in pronominal systems

* Pemon-Panare \**(j)u(-rə)-to* for <span class='smallcaps'>1+2</span>
* Pemon-Panare /j/ in first person? (not in [Language mac](LanguageTable?#cldf:mac) and Kapón)
* formal developments of <span class='smallcaps'>1+3</span> pronoun in Parukotoan, Pekodian, and Taranoan
